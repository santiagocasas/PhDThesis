\begin{abstract}
\addchaptertocentry{\abstractname} % Add the abstract to the table of contents

This thesis investigates the importance of non-linear structure formation on the 
the determination of the cosmological parameters for models beyond the standard $\lcdm$ scenario.
One of the main motivations is that future galaxy surveys will be able to measure the positions
and shapes of approximately $10^7$ galaxies and will be 
able to determine with high precision their two-point correlation function, 
or its equivalent in wavevector space, the matter power spectrum. 
If one is able to extract the information encoded in the power spectrum at small scales,
one can gain a substantial constraining power on the cosmological parameters.
In this dissertation I look at different models of Dark Energy and Modified Gravity:
Coupled Dark Energy, Growing Neutrino Quintessence, Effective Field Theory, Horndeski models,
and general phenomenological parameterizations of Modified Gravity. 
To study non-linear structure formation I use standard and specialized N-body simulations, 
semi-analytic prescriptions based on the Halo model
and resummation methods in cosmological perturbation theory.
In order to constrain the model parameters and to forecast the errors measured by future surveys,
I use the Bayesian Fisher matrix formalism, for which I developed a code that can deal with
Galaxy Clustering and Weak Lensing observables.
The overall result of this work is that non-linear structure formation
cannot be neglected, if one pretends to discriminate between general Dark Energy models and
the Cosmological Constant scenario in the near future.



\end{abstract}
