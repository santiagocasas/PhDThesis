\begin{abstract}
\addchaptertocentry{\abstractname} % Add the abstract to the table of contents

We investigate in this thesis the importance of non-linear structure formation on the 
determination of cosmological parameters for models beyond the standard $\lcdm$ scenario.
A major motivation therefore is the ability of future galaxy surveys to measure the positions
and shapes of approximately $10^7$ galaxies and precisely determine their two-point correlation function, 
or its equivalent in wavevector space, the galaxy power spectrum. 
If one is capable to extract the information encoded in the power spectrum at small scales,
one can gain a substantial constraining power on the parameters of the underlying theory.
This dissertation considers different models beyond $\lcdm$:
Coupled Dark Energy, Growing Neutrino Quintessence, Effective Field Theory, Horndeski theory
and general phenomenological parameterizations of Modified Gravity. 
In this work we study non-linear structure formation with a variety of different methodologies:
fitting formulae, semi-analytic prescriptions based on the Halo model, resummation methods in 
cosmological perturbation theory and specialized N-body simulations.
In order to forecast how well future surveys are able to constrain the cosmological parameters,
we use the Bayesian Fisher matrix formalism, for which we developed a code designed to handle
Galaxy Clustering and Weak Lensing observables.
We obtain new results on linear and non-linear forecasts for Modified Gravity parameterizations
with next-generation surveys, namely Euclid, SKA and DESI.
We also determine the best combination of redshift-binned Modified Gravity parameters constrained by these upcoming surveys.
Furthermore, we present the first forecasts on Coupled Dark Energy performed with fitting formulae obtained from N-body simulations.
In these forecasts we find that including non-linear scales improves by more than an order of 
magnitude the constraints obtained with linear theory only.
For the Growing Neutrino Quintessence model we use special non-Newtonian N-body simulations. With them we are able to show that this model presents different regimes for the dynamics of neutrino lumps and that the lump interactions induce a heating of the neutrino fluid. 
Moreover, we obtain the non-linear corrections to the power spectrum in the case of Horndeski models assuming the quasistatic regime. To achieve this, we apply a resummation method for higher order perturbation theory, which had so far only been used within the standard cosmological model.
The main message of this thesis is that non-linear structure formation has a significant impact on cosmology and if we want to discriminate between competing theories, using forthcoming data, 
we need to take non-linearities into account and understand their implications.



\end{abstract}
